Living in Russia vs USA: Pros and Cons

I've had the pleasure of experiencing everyday life on two very different continents, and I believe it's time to reflect.

Having spent over 13 years living in various US states and around 15 years in Russia, I've engaged in hundreds of conversations with people from all walks of life about the quality of life in these places. My diverse upbringing includes working different jobs and receiving education in both Russia and the US. I aim to provide a fair and balanced critique, as I don't consider myself biased toward either country. However, I recognize that there are many people with different experiences and opinions, which is perfectly normal. Here, I will discuss strictly my personal experiences. These observations are filtered through my own worldview and cannot be entirely objective.

Although I have some political opinions, this discussion won't delve deeply into the political choices of either country. Instead, it will focus on factors that shape the life of an average working-class person.

Each point I mention could easily be expanded into a separate article, but due to limited time, I'll leave them open to interpretation. I also won't mention pros and cons that are similar in both countries. Here are the pros and cons in a nutshell:

America

Pros:

  • People desire and value freedom.
  • Sequoia trees.
  • Easy to strike up conversations.
  • Different laws in each state.
  • Guns.
  • Top-notch movies, music and style.
  • World-renowned language.
  • Valuable passport for travel.
  • Developed platforms for artists and content creators.
  • Decent benefits and infrastructure for the disabled.

Cons:

  • No free healthcare.
  • No freedom of speech as advertised.
  • Dystopian capitalism.
  • Poor-tasting groceries.
  • Idiots with guns.
  • Cancel culture.
  • High cost of living.
  • Work-oriented culture.
  • Bad education.
  • Strange obsession with democracy.
  • Infrastructure dependent on cars.
  • Mandatory car insurance.
  • Eating out as a common form of recreation.
  • The tipping system.
  • No free college.
  • Occassional mass hysteria.
  • Wild hurricanes.
  • Provider-locked phones.
  • Lack of authenticity.
  • The big pharma monopoly.
  • On-edge cops.

Russia

Pros:

  • Free healthcare.
  • Layed back work culture.
  • Affordable rent and groceries.
  • Decent public transportation in major cities.
  • Good education that can be free.
  • Beautiful women.
  • No gay parades.
  • Piracy of just about anything digital.

Cons:

  • Depressing brutalist scenery.
  • Harsh winters.
  • Angry neighbouring countries.
  • Common rudeness.
  • Widespread corruption.
  • Nothing made for the disabled.
  • Cringe movies.
  • Prohibition on carrying self-defence items on the subway.
  • Gulag like prisons.
  • Dominant orthodox church influence.
  • Unregulated rental scooters downtown.
  • Mandatory military service for young men.
  • Unreliable internet providers.
  • Lazy cops.
